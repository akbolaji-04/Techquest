1. Generation of Computer

In ancienttimes, the early man was a hunter and a flesh eater. He never had anything to count, but as time passed man started to think of cultivation of crops and rearing of farm animals.
Then, arose the need for him to count. Because of this, the early man began using the following devices to count numbers.

  • Fingers
  • Stones
  • Sticks
  • Cowries

Fingers

The ancient people made use of fingers to count.
Fingers can only be used to count digits from 1 to 10. And as time passed, the fingers and toes were used to count numbers more than 10

Stones

As a result of the stress of using fngers to count numbers of animals larger than 20.
The early man learnt how to use stones and pebbles for counting and also to count his wealth

Sticks

Man later used sticks to count and perform addition and subtraction.
This technique also requires physical effort and a lot of stress in terms of performing larger calculation.

Cowries

Cowries were also used to count and perform simple arithmetic in ancient time.
This technique also took too much energy, especially when large numbers were involved.

Mechanical Counting Devices

As societies advanced, mechanical devices like the abacus were invented to aid in counting. Later, tools such as the slide rule, logarithmic instruments, and the Pascal calculator enhanced calculation capabilities.

  • Abacus
  • Slide Rule
  • Logarithm

Abacus

An Abacus is a mechanical counting device which has a wooden frame. Around the year 1200 AD, the chinese used Abacus which was made of colored beads threaded on a string. The beads were threaded on lines of wire frame. Tje beands on the first wire counted the units, the beads on the second wire counted the tens, the beads on the third wire counted the hundreds and so on.
By moving the beads back and forth along wires, numbers could be added, subtracted, multiplied, etc.

Slide Rule

The slide rule is a calculating device that was based on the principles of logarithms. It is used for calculation which incolved multiplication and division. It was originated by Williams Qugthred in 1621.
The use of slide rule is only accurate to three or four decimal places. It was also used by engineers and scientists for extraction of note and calculation of numbers raised to powers.

Logarithm

Logarithms are tabulated in rows and columns to add, substract and multiply basic sums. John Napier, a Scottish mathematician, invented the logarithm in 1614. The logarithm is a mathematical concept that simplifies multiplication and division.
Logarithms are used to solve complex mathematical problems and are the basis for the slide rule.

An electronic counting device is a device that uses electronic circuitry to count events or items. This broad category encompasses a wide range of devices with varying levels of complexity and applications. Here's a breakdown of key aspects:
There are several electronic counting devices which include:

  • Herman Hollerith’s Punchcards
  • John Von Neumann’s Machine
  • Modern Machine

Herman Hollerith’s Punchcards

Herman Hollerith invented machine which worked on electricity for the first time in 1889. One machine was used for recording data and the other was used for doing calculations of the recorded data. His machine was capable of reading both numbers and letters.
Hollerith invented the punch card, a card with holes punched in it to represent data. The punch card was used to input data into the machine, which then processed the data and produced results.
